Salesperson Reports

Reports that are intended for salespeople use fall into two categories: general reports that show all the data, and reports that show only the data of the signed in salesperson. The following is a list of reports intended for use by salespeople for each category, with a brief description of each report. 


Note: Not all users will necessarily have all of the reports that are listed. The available reports are determined by the Report Set assigned to each user. For more information on Report Sets, please see the User Options Screen help document.

General Reports


Avails: Produces a listing of the available inventory per week for the selected vehicles using the entered start date.


Avails Pressure: Lists the total inventory per vehicle by daypart, and the amount of available inventory if selected proposals became orders and were scheduled.


Rate Card: Lists one or more rate cards for the selected year and vehicles, with the rates defined for each vehicle/daypart. This report can be run to show totals by week, month, or quarter.

Reports that filter by Salesperson


Ageing by Salesperson: Produces an ageing by each salesperson. This report can be run for cash, trade, merchandising, or promotion dollars.


Audience Delivery: Looks at all the spots aired on an order within a specified date span, and compares the actual audience delivered, to the audience promised on the proposal.


Billed and Booked: Produces a report of the entire year's advertising dollars. Past month figures are obtained from billing, future months figures are obtained from business on the books. 


Billed and Booked Comparisons: Compares up to 12 months of this year’s totals vs. last year’s totals, based on a broadcast, corporate or regular calendar. 


Billed and Booked Recap: This can be generated by corporate or standard month, with revenue reported in gross, net, or net-net figures for a 12 month period breaking out commission vs. direct dollars. It will automatically break order revenue out by sales source.  When requested by corporate month, all revenue figures come from business on books, when requested by standard broadcast month, historical figures will come from the billing and future revenue is from the business on books.


CPP/CPM by 30’’ Units: Produce CPPs or CPMs for 30" units by vehicle or advertiser. The generated CPP/CPMs will be obtained from spot data, using the contracts primary demo, or a selected demo for all spots. Up to 12 monthly values are printed for the standard broadcast month.


CPP/CPM by Demo: Summarizes all orders by the primary demo category for the total year and each of the four quarters. Each line includes the primary demo, vehicle, advertiser/product, office, total number of thirty second units, gross dollars, average rate, and quarterly CPP or CPM.


CPP/CPM by Vehicle: Calculates the CPP or CPM for each daypart within a vehicle. Up to thirteen demographics can be selected to see if demographic goals are being met. The average rate card price and associated book is also printed.


Daily Sales Activity by Contract: This report lists all order activity within a specific date range, and shows all new activity, as well as increases and decreases in dollars. Any comments entered in the Change Reason field in the order screen will be displayed. The report can be sorted alphabetically by advertiser or sales office.


Daily Sales Activity by Month: This report lists all order activity within a specific date range broken out by monthly totals based on the original entry date or the date the contract was modified. The report can be generated by a calendar or standard broadcast month. Data can be sorted a variety of ways, and can include or exclude Air Time, NTR, and Hard Cost revenue.


Margin Acquisition: Compares acquisition costs to net revenue to arrive at a margin percent to determine if a contract and/or vehicle is profitable or not.


NTR Billed and Booked: Shows NTR revenue for a 12 month period with quarterly and annual totals. For standard broadcast  reporting, past revenue is pulled from billing (from invoices and adjustments), while future revenue is pulled from business on books. Corporate reporting only pulls invoice adjustments for past months, all other revenue (past and future) comes from business on books.


NTR Recap: Used to verify NTR data is entered correctly and fulfilled properly. The information is requested by the scheduled bill date, and can be sorted by advertiser, NTR type, schedule bill date, salesperson, or vehicle. You can choose whether or not to include the NTR description on a separate line, as well as including or excluding Hard Cost items and displaying acquisition costs.


Paperwork Summary: Lists holds, orders, and proposals (working, incomplete, and complete) defined in the system. Standard, Reserved, Remnant, Direct Response, Per Inquiry, PSA, Promo, Cash, and Trade contracts can all be selected or deselected. Any contract with an input gross that doesn't match the total gross of the contract will be noted as discrepant. This report can be run by contract or by line.


Post Buy Analysis: Total net impressions for a contract are compared against the guaranteed impressions and broken out for paid spots, makegood spots, and bonus spots, based on the audience percentage associated with a region. Over/Under Delivery differences can be highlighted, and can be included or excluded for makegood and/or Bonus spots.


Proposals/Contracts: Produces a printout of the selected contracts or proposals. Differences Only can be run to show only the lines revised since the last modification of an order. “Printables” prints all orders that haven't yet been printed.


Research Revenue: Lists all revenue generated from scheduled spots with research data, and is intended to be generated on a monthly basis.


Sales Analysis by CPP & CPM: Calculates the CPP and CPM values for any or all demo categories obtained from the primary demo entered on all contracts within the selected dates. Separate columns are used to show totals including remnants, direct response, and per inquiry contracts, and excluding such contracts.


Sales Pricing Analysis: Shows actual dollars vs. rate card dollars. Totals are given by contract, salesperson, office and overall.


Weekly Sales Activity by Month: Reports any modifications - increases and/or decreases to previously existing contracts, or new contracts - made within a selected week for a twelve month period selected by the user. Modifications are reflected by increases and decreases from the previous week.


Weekly Sales Activity by Quarter: Reports any modifications - increases and/or decreases to previously existing contracts, or new contracts - made within a selected week for the selected quarter. Modifications are reflected by increases and decreases from the previous week by a quarterly period.
