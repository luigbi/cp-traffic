Installment

Installment Billing lets sales define a bill amount for each invoice cycle, regardless of how the spots, NTR, or multimedia is ordered. 


  • When an Installment Order is printed, no prices show for any spots or NTR items.
  • The Billing (Installment) Summary page shows the Installment amounts to bill (includes airtime and NTR prices).
  • When an Installment Invoice is printed, no rates show for the spots or NTR items.
  • If the Bill Cycle is Calendar or Weekly, Installment Billing is disabled.
  • Orders are defined as installment billing prior to the first billing on the order.  They cannot be changed once billing has been generated.

Important information about NTR and agency commissions

When entering a contract that will use the Installment Billing feature, if the advertiser has an agency and is NTR only, the NTR must either all be set to "agency commissionable = yes" or "agency commissionable = no", there cannot be a mix of the two commission settings for a single contract. 


If the advertiser has an agency and there is both NTR and Air Time on the contract, the NTR commission setting must be set to Yes. (If the advertiser is a direct advertiser, then the NTR agency commissionable setting will be set to No automatically. This is true whether there is NTR only or NTR and Air Time on the contract.)

Allocating Installment Billing

The total gross and the contract dollars must be in balance prior to accessing the Installment billing tab. If the Installment tab is pressed while out of balance, the following message will appear: “Gross amount and total line amount plus NTR not in balance”. The contract must be in balance before defining the installment amounts.


The picture below shows an example of allocated installment billing where the entire invoice amount has been allocated to the first month of the contract.

The installment billing screen has two parts: a header area, and a grid below it.


Installment Header Area

Starting Invoice Date: This is the first month that you would like to begin invoicing the order. The first billing date of the flight will be shown, but you can select any billing date with the flight from the dropdown when allocating installment billing.


# Months: The number of months you wish to generate invoices. This does not mean you will generate an invoice every month, but you must to enter the total number of months between the generation of the first and last invoice (1 if you are billing up front or at the end of flight).


End Invoice Date: The final invoice date. Any date from the first billing date to the last billing month can be selected from the dropdown (the number of months may change depending on the date selected).


# Months On/# Months Off: If there’s a pattern to the monthly invoice generation, for example, where you will generate one invoice every three months, you can use these fields to create that frequency. In the example pictured above, there was one month on, and two months off, so that the entire invoice amount would be automatically allocated to the first invoice month (for a three month contract.)


After filling out the header area, press the Generate button to create the “To Invoice” amounts in the grid area based on the header options. 

Installment Grid Area

Vehicle: Each vehicle on the contract is listed, with one row for each invoice month.


Date: The invoice date.


Ordered: The Ordered revenue amount.


Revenue: Total revenue amount per invoice month.


To Invoice: The amount that will be invoiced per vehicle per month based on the installment billing options that were selected in the header. This value can be changed to meet client billing needs, as long as the installment billing remains in balance and before the first invoice.


Invoice/Month: The total amount that will be invoiced per month.


The blue column headers can be clicked on to sort the grid by vehicle or date.


The Total gross amount and billed amount from the “To Invoice” column must be in balance; if there is a discrepancy, the totals in the bottom right will be shown in red and you will not be able to save the contract until the discrepancy has been resolved.
