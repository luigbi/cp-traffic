Planner - Proposals and Orders

There are several types of proposal/order types. This gives management reporting flexibility and traffic scheduling flexibility. For example, the Avails and Billed and Booked Reports have the option to include or exclude any of the types. The Average Spot Price Report has the ability to exclude Direct Response and Per Inquiry revenue, not remnant.  


For scheduling examples, you may want to guarantee a specific number of spots for a ‘call in response’ type of commercial.  The Direct Response type allows you to do it.  The Per Inquiry type allows you to create the spots as needed to fill unsold avails.

Proposal/Order Types 

Standard

“Standard” is what is used for any general buy that has air time, NTR, and/or multimedia.  A standard buy can be classified as a “Hold” or “Order.” Standard Order spots are created automatically during scheduling, and Standard spots appear on the invoice/affidavit.

Reservation Contracts

Reservation Contracts allow inventory to be held until the actual contract is entered. Reservation spots are highlighted in yellow on the Spots screen. Once the expected contracts are received, the reservation spots are replaced with the paid spots. Salespeople are prevented from knowing that a booked avail is booked with a reservation spot.

Key Features about Reservations

  • Reservation spots reserve avails so that they can be held and sold at a later date
    • The Demo Bar and Avails report will reflect Reservation Contracts
  • It is recommended that a “Reservation” advertiser and agency be entered and used for reservation contracts
  • Reservation spots should be entered with no spot prices
  • Reservation spots should all be replaced, canceled, or hidden before the log is generated
    • The Log Check has the option to choose “reservation” to see if there are any reservation spots before the logs are finalized. Traffic should be instructed to run the Check and let you know if there are any reservation spots
  • Paperwork Summary and Quarterly Booked Spots can be generated to include Reservation Contracts

Remnant 

This is a special kind of order that may or may not get a Proposal. Remnant contracts are for those spots that are sold after all upfront and scatter buys are booked. Typically, the remaining inventory is released and the planners negotiate the remnant rates for the remaining inventory.

Direct Response

This is a special kind of order that may or may not get a Proposal. Direct Response orders generally are for those commercials that ask you to ‘call in’ to buy their product. Generally there is a spot cost assigned to each commercial aired.  The spots are not scheduled by the system; they are manually created in the Spots Screen by dragging the advertiser to an unsold avail. Only aired spots are billed; missed and canceled spots are ignored.   

Per Inquiry

This is similar to a Direct Response but typically no spot costs are known at the time the PI order is entered. It is scheduled manually, and the advertiser pays based on responses received when the commercial is aired, rather than at a specific spot price. 


  1. PIs will produce a zero dollar invoice
  2. The advertiser will send a check based on the number of calls they received
  3. When received, enter an invoice adjustment against the zero dollar invoice for the full amount of the check 
  4. Post the check against the adjusted invoice  

PSA

This is designed for use by Traffic to enter Public Service Announcements. Spots for PSA contracts can be automatically scheduled into PSA avails or manually scheduled into Contract Avails in the Spots Screen.  PSA spots can also be created to blackout commercial spots from the Log Screen.

Promos 

This is designed for Traffic to enter Promotional Announcements. Spots for Promo contracts can be automatically scheduled into Promo avails or manually scheduled into Contract Avails. 

Proposal Status

During the Proposal negotiation process, several versions of a single proposal can be created (all sharing one proposal number) to present to the agency. 


Once the proposal is completed, sales can switch the status from Working Proposal to Complete Proposal, and an Alert is sent to the Sales Manager. 


Once a version is accepted by the agency, the Sales Manager can select that version by going into the Proposal through the “Change an Existing Proposal” task, selecting the version from the “Ver #” dropdown, and change its status to be Approved Order or Hold. When this change is saved, all other versions of the proposal are deleted, only the accepted version is saved. 


As an example, if proposal 100 had four versions presented and the agency accepted version 2, when sales management selected Completed proposal 100, version 2 (from the Ver # dropdown), and changed it to an Approved Order, version 1, 3, and 4 would be deleted.
