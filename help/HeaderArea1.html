Header Area

The contract header is the section in the upper half of the screen that contains general information about the contract, such as the advertiser, product, agency, and so on. 

The following is an explanation of each contract header field. Not every field described below will be shown on every system, as some are only displayed when activated in Traffic Site Options.


Status: The status field is used to indicate what state the order is in, with the following possible options being available: approved hold and approved order.


Type: The different contract types are explained below.


Standard: spots from the contract will be scheduled and automatically placed in the Spots screen by the system.


Reservation: used if an advertiser wants to reserve inventory over a large date span, but does not know yet what products it will be for. Once the actual order/product is determined, the inventory is easily “transferred” to the actual contract by indicating the Reservation Order/Advertiser in the order number field of the action order. Typically Reservation contracts go in at zero dollars given that they are used for reserving inventory.


Remnant: these orders generally are at a lower rate and have a lower priority than a standard order. A setting in Site Options determines whether remnant spots are automatically or manually scheduled. 


Direct Response: Direct Response orders are for those commercials that ask consumers to call in to buy the product. Generally there is a spot cost assigned to each commercial aired. The spots are not scheduled by the system, but created manually by dragging the advertiser to an unsold avail.


Per Inquiry: similar to the Direct Response type, it’s also scheduled manually, but is paid based on responses received when the commercial is aired, rather than at a specific spot price. The client receives a specific sum of revenue per each call generated by the commercial aired.


PSA: used to enter Public Service Announcements. Spots for PSA contracts can be automatically scheduled into PSA avails or manually scheduled depending on how it’s configured in Traffic Site Options. 


Promo: used to enter Promotional Announcements. Spots for Promo contracts can be automatically scheduled into Promo avails or manually scheduled depending on how it’s configured in Traffic Site Options.  


Important note about contract types: the contract type cannot be altered once the order is saved and scheduled. If the type must be changed, the original order must be canceled and a new one entered with the correct type. 


Order Number: the order number field consists of the following:


Number: The contract number is assigned (or you can enter your own) when you save it. After saving, the contract number will be shown in the “Number” field. 


Agency Estimate #: the agency’s internal order number. If entered, it will appear on the invoice sent to the agency. 

Reserved Advertiser and Order #: When entering an order that references a reservation order, select the reservation advertiser and reservation order number.


Advertiser: this field shows the contract advertiser. When entering a new contract, either an existing advertiser can be selected from the advertiser list, or a new advertiser can be entered if needed. 

To enter a new advertiser:

1. Select “New” from the advertiser dropdown and press Tab. This will bring up the new advertiser screen.

2. Enter the advertiser name and abbreviation, as shown below.


 

3. Set the Political toggle to No or Yes (if used).

4. At this point, if the new advertiser uses an agency and is not a direct advertiser, press Save and then Done to return to the main Orders screen. If the advertiser is a direct advertiser, press the “Direct” radio button and enter the contract address, the billing address (if different from the contract address), the Address ID (used to identify the advertiser city for reporting purposes), and the default buyer, and then press Save and Done to add the new advertiser.


Product: the contract product is shown in this field. When entering a new contract, the product last associated with the advertiser (the default product) will appear. You can select a different product from the dropdown, or enter a new product by typing it in.


Agency: shows the contract agency, or “direct advertiser” if the contract advertiser is a direct advertiser. When entering a new contract, either an existing agency can be selected from here, or a new agency can be added. 

To enter a new agency:

1. Select “New” from the agency dropdown and press Tab. This will bring up the new agency screen.

2. Enter the agency name, abbreviation, City ID, and set the Active/Dormant toggle to Active.

3. Enter the contract address and billing address if needed. The buyer information can also be entered.

4. Press Save and then press Done to return to the main screen.


Salesperson: when entering a new contract when signed in as a salesperson, the salesperson name will be defaulted to that salesperson. Additional salespeople can be entered if the commission is split, along with the commission percentage. In the example below, there’s a 75% and 25% split between the two salespeople.

Up to 10 salespeople can be entered on a contract.


Product Protection: product protection codes help keep like products apart from each other during the scheduling process. Up to two product protection codes can be entered. This is a mandatory field on some system configurations, and optional on others.



Program Exclusions: for certain types of programs that the advertiser is not allowed to air on (not pictured).


Business Category: used to group advertisers into categories for reporting purposes. 


Buyer and Buyer’s Phone Number: are defaulted from the agency screen. If the buyer is different from the default, you can select the buyer from the dropdown, or enter a new buyer by selecting New, pressing Tab, and filling out the name (required) and other fields (optional).


Share of Budget: when enabled, this field is used to enter the percentage of the advertiser’s budget your network is receiving and the percentage that your competitors are receiving. 


Merchandising: if a contract contains merchandising as part of the contract, a merchandising comment must be entered in this field. The dollars must be distributed before the order can be saved. 

To enter a merchandising comment:

1. From the merchandising field, select “Comment” and press Tab.

2. Select the predefined merchandising percentage from the upper left corner of the screen and press the Move button to move the comment to the lower half of the merchandising comment screen.

3. Press Tab to exit the screen.

Promotional: if a contract contains promotional consideration as part of the contract, a promotional comment must be entered in this field. The dollars must be distributed before the order can be saved.

To enter a promotional comment:

1. From the promotion field, select “Comment” and press Tab.

2. Select the predefined promotional percentage from the upper left corner of the screen and press the Move button to move the comment to the lower half of the promotion comment screen.

3. Press Tab to exit the screen.

Revenue Sets: when used, Revenue Sets are different ways of grouping business for reporting purposes. Examples: Upfront or Scatter, and New or Return. Other categories may also be used when defined in the system. 


Segment: the text entered in this field appears on log format C80 (not pictured). 


Demographics: up to four demo categories can be entered in this field. The price type can be toggled between CPP or CPM by clicking the “Demo Price Type” toggle. 


If “Show Impressions on Proposal” is checked in the Traffic Site Options -> Audience tab, the option “Show Impressions” will appear here. If this toggle is set to “Yes”, the word “Impressions” will be shown in the demo field of the Proposals/Contracts report, contract snapshot, and on the Insertion Orders report, instead of the chosen demo category. In addition, instead of showing “avg aud” (average audience) in the column headers of the report, it will show “download”. If “Show ‘Impressions’” is set to “No”, any defined demo categories will be shown on the report instead.


Delivery Guarantee %: this commits the network to airing a percentage of spots within the ordered daypart, therefore it’s the minimum GRIMPs guaranteed to be delivered.


Trade: sets the trade percentage for the contract, and can be set to any value between zero and one hundred. If set to zero, it is an all cash contract. If set to 100%, then it’s an all trade contract. If set to any other value, it is a split cash/trade order. Split cash/trade orders will produce two sets of invoices, one labeled Cash that shows the cash percentage, and one labeled Trade that shows the trade percentage.


Rate Card: defaults to the most current rate card. The rate card is used to populate the list of selectable vehicles and dayparts.


Total Gross: shows the total air time and NTR (if used) gross dollars as input manually. 


Billing Cycle: the available billing cycles are standard, calendar, and weekly. On most systems, only standard is used and the billing cycle field is not shown. If the billing cycle is calendar or weekly, then Installment Billing is disabled.

Comment Area

Comments: the four comment fields are used to enter different types of comments.

 

Internal Comment: this comment only appears on the order and is used to making internal notes. 


Cancellation Clause: if there is a standard policy select it or enter whatever terms are needed in the text field. Note: on some configurations, entering a cancellation clause is mandatory and a warning sound will play when trying to tab past this field without entering it.


Change Reason: either select a standard change reason comment or enter the information in the text field. If a change reason has been entered and a contract is changed, the previously defined change reason will be cleared. 


Other Comment: can be used to add an additional comment if needed. 

To enter a comment in any of the comment fields:

1. Click in the Comment field, click the dropdown arrow, select “Comment”, and press Tab.

2. If a pre-defined comment exists in the upper left corner and should be used, select it, then press the Move button to move it to the lower half of the screen. Then press Tab to exit.

3. If a pre-defined comment doesn’t exist, or custom comment text is needed, simply type in the text field in the lower half of the screen to add custom comment text.

4. The checkboxes are used to select where the comment will appear. In the example below, the Cancellation Comment will appear on the printed proposal and order. The other checkboxes make the comment appear on the Insertion Orders report, on the Spots and Copy screen, and on the invoices. Not all options are available for each comment type.
