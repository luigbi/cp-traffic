Invoice Adjustments

Invoice Adjustments (ANs) are used to increase or decrease revenue. Adjustments are aged to the invoice date. Adjustments can be used to:

  • Increase or decrease an invoice per client/sales request
  • Add revenue for Per Inquiry type contracts so you can post the cash received
  • Correct an invoice when you invoiced too little or too much
  • Correct an invoice if you invoiced a client with an agency when they were supposed to be direct or vice versa


A Credit/Debit Memo can be created for adjustments that you have applied to an invoice. The Memo report shows one line per transaction per date with any comments that you may have entered. The estimate number and taxes, if applicable, will also show on the memo.


Reports that include the adjustment to revenue are:

  • The aging shows the adjustment aged to the invoice date
  • The Sales Commission shows the adjustment based on the transaction date
  • The Invoice Register shows the adjustment based on the transaction date
  • The Detail Tie Out report shows the adjustment based on the transaction date
  • The Billed and Booked report has a question in Site Options to determine how the adjustment should be reflected

Invoice Adjustment Methods

Adjustments can be applied to any open transaction in A/R. If the invoice has multiple transactions you can select one of three options that best suits how the revenue should be adjusted. You can apply the adjustment proportionally to the invoiced amounts, to specific billing vehicles or to specific airing vehicles. This allows you to affect the revenue for the individiual vehicle/participant if needed.


You can apply an AN by:

  • Invoice: This groups together all vehicles on the IN per invoice.  When you enter an amount to adjust, it will proportionally apply the adjustment transactions to the invoice
  • Billing Vehicle: This groups together all IN transactions with the same billing vehicle to allow you to adjust a portion of an invoice
  • Airing Vehicle: This lists each transaction individually allowing you to adjust any element of an invoice


To select the option to enter the adjustment revenue, click “Adj Inv” to change it to “Adj Bill Veh” or “Adj Air Veh”.

Entering an Adjustment

Applying an AN by Invoice

  1. Select the agency and the Invoice Adjustment action 
  2. Enter the Transaction Date
  3. Scroll down the list of transactions until you see the correct “Due” column for the invoice
  4. Click in the “Adj Inv” column next to the Due column
  5. The Due column will change to $.00 and the “Adj Inv” amount will change to credit the invoice
  6. The System will reduce the IN by the total amount due, you can highlight the dollars and change the +/- amount
  7. The Adj Gross will show the gross amount that will be adjusted
  8. You can attach a comment to the AN if needed
  9. The Total Adj Net will list the total net revenue to be adjusted
  10. The Total Adj will list the total gross revenue to be adjusted
  11. Save will create an AN transaction line item for the Adjustment

Applying an AN by Billing Vehicle 

An Invoice may have multiple transactions/packages, causing the need to adjust a portion of the invoice. “Adj Bill Veh” will allow to apply your adjustment to a portion of the invoice.  


  1. Select “Adj Bill Veh”
  2. Enter the Transaction Date
  3. Click in the “Adj Bill Veh” column for the vehicle amount that will be adjusted
  4. You must click on a line that has an amount due
  5. The Due column will change to $.00 and the “Adj Bill Veh” amount will change to credit the invoice
  6. The System will reduce the IN by the total amount due, you can highlight the dollars and change the +/- amount
  7. If the amount needs to be changed, mouse click on the amount in the “Adj Bill Veh” column and enter the correct amount
  8. Tab will take you to the Comment Field where you can enter a comment to this adjustment for reference purposes

Applying an AN by Airing Vehicle

If your invoice has multiple transactions/vehicles, you may need to apply and adjustment to one vehicle of that invoice. By changing the toggle to “Adj Air Veh”, you will easily be able to apply your adjustment to any item of the invoice.

 

  1. Select “Adj Air Veh”
  2. Enter the Transaction Date
  3. Click in the “Adj Air Veh” column for the vehicle amount that will be adjusted
  4. You must click on a line that has an amount due
  5. If the amount needs to be changed, mouse click on the amount in the “Adj Air Veh” column and enter the correct amount
  6. The system will reduce the IN by the total amount due, you can highlight the dollars and change the +/- amount
  7. Tab will take you to the Comment Field where you can enter a comment to this adjustment for reference purposes

Adjusting Agency Commissions 

If an invoice was billed as direct when it should have had an agency, or with an agency commission when it should have been direct, you can correct the error with an Invoice Adjustment (AN). The Net amounts will only be altered with this procedure. 


After selecting the client, Invoice Adjustment, and entering the transaction date, click in the Adjust Invoice (Adj Inv) column. The total amount due will appear. 


Enter a credit if deducting an agency commission amount. Example:  -3480.00, or a debit if adding the erroneously deducted commission from an invoice that should have been billed direct.


Press Tab, the comment box will appear where you can define the reason for the adjustment. This comment will appear on the Credit/Debit memo, the Invoice Register and the Aging.

 

Tab into the Adjusted Gross (Adj Gross) column, and enter 0, since only the net will be affected. 


Once saved the invoice adjustments will be allocated to each vehicle on the invoice, and the totals at the bottom will reflect the net revenue change.  

Statement Report
