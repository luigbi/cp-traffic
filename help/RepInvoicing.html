Rep Invoicing

Rep vehicles do not have scheduled spots like the conventional or selling vehicles.  Rep vehicle orders have a ‘count’ of the number of spots that are supposed to air for a specific daypart.  When the Rep vehicle is posted for the month, the true airing spot count is entered.


Rep invoicing is a three part process:

  1. The ordered revenue is updated into account receivables when Gen Rep A/R is run.  At this time, there is no paperwork to send to the client. 
  2. The aired spots are posted in Post Log from the information provided by the station or producer.
  3. The invoice to the client is generated when Print Rep Invoices is run, and any differences between the aired and ordered are reflected automatically with adjustments.

Updating Accounts Receivables

  1. Gen Rep A/R creates the Invoice transactions for the ordered Rep dollars.  When you create Final Rep Invoices, if the final amount to bill differs from what was ordered, billing adjustments will be automatically calculated and created.
  2. The invoice numbers on the adjustments will match the original invoice numbers created when the Gen Rep A/R was done.  
  3. The adjustments will be “aged” to the month of the original invoice, whenever an Ageing report is run.

Spot Prices

  1. The system bills using the spot prices on the Rep order.  
  2. Makegood spots are billed at the rate for the line they are entered on the Posting Screen.
  3. It is important that the posting person pays attention to the spot rate when you are posting the spots.  The number of spots that you define to air will be billed at the ordered rate.
  4. For those markets that are imported, you need to ensure that for those cases where there are multiple ordered lines for the same vehicle, that the number of spots airing is posted to the vehicle with the correct spot price.

Gen Rep

The first step to the monthly billing is to select “Gen Rep A/R”. This generates your revenue figures and assigns an invoice number to each order for the month without printing any invoices.  These amounts are based on a standard broadcast month. 


Gen Rep should be selected at the same time that you generate your final month end billing for Air Time and NTR.



When you have completed the Gen Rep A/R, you can get your revenue totals from one of the Invoice Register Reports.  * Counterpoint recommends using one of the following Invoice Registers for your reporting totals.


* Invoice Register by Number (summary)

Detail - Airing or Billing vehicle, numerical by invoice number, lists all transactions and invoice totals by vehicle.  Shows the billing and airing vehicle.  

Summary – Numeric by invoice, one line per contract.


*Invoice Register by Airing Vehicle  - Vehicle Group Selectivity

Detail – Alphabetically by vehicle.  Lists every transaction and vehicle totals. 

Summary - One line per vehicle group selected.  Automatically breaks outs sales sources.


*Invoice Register by Sales Source

Detail - Alphabetic by advertiser listing all transactions for the order.

Summary – One line per contract. 


Invoice Register by Advertiser

Detail - Alphabetic by advertiser, one line per contract.

Summary - One line per advertiser, automatically breaks out sales sources.


Invoice Register by Agency 

Detail - Alphabetic by agency, one line per contract.

Summary Alphabetic by Agency - one line per agency, automatically breaks out sales sources.

 

Invoice Register by Salesperson

Detail - Shows one line per contract, salesperson within office.  

Summary – Shows one line totals by salesperson within office.

 

Invoice Register by Billing Vehicle

Detail - Alphabetically by billing vehicle.  Lists every transaction and vehicle totals.

Summary - Alphabetic by billing vehicle totals.  Automatically breaks out sales source.

 

Invoice Register by Office/Vehicle

Detail – Sorts by sales source, lists each sales office and the advertiser within the airing vehicle.  

Summary - Lists each sales office, lists totals by airing vehicle & totals by office.
