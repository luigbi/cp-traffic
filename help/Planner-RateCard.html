Planner - Rate Card

The Rate Card Screen is where the vehicles, dayparts, and shows that are sold, along with their prices. Every proposal or order defined in the system references a Rate Card.  Rate Cards are also used for various analysis reports.


You can enter as many different rate cards for a year as you want. For example, in addition to entering a basic rate card for the year, you may decide to enter a minimum rate card that can be used for remnant type contracts or similar spot types. The effectiveness of Rate Cards can be measured by comparing them to other Rate Cards, Actuals, Budgets, the prices needed to make budget or research data. If any Rate Cards are defined whose number begins with a “~”, those Rate Cards will not be shown to the Salespeople when entering proposals or orders.  This allows you to create a Rate Card only for reporting purposes.


The prices that are entered for a rate card can be changed seasonally, by irregular intervals or by week.  Premiums and discounts can be specified to allow automatic price adjustment for billboards, fixed times, spot lengths other than 30 seconds, orders buying many spots per week, many weeks, specific week days or partial dayparts.  

Rate Card Impact Button

The Impact screen is used to assess the effect of proposals on the current budget or plan. It compares the Rate Card price to the Proposal price, calculates the difference, and determines the rate at which you must sell the remaining inventory to make plan.

Using the impact screen

Select the budget or plan that you will be comparing the proposals to, then select any or all proposals. You can choose any or all versions of a proposal. Each proposal displays the proposal number, version number, and status of the proposal, product name, the start date of the proposal, the total number of weeks, and the internal comment (if one is defined for the proposal).


Reading the Impact Screen

The Impact Screen can by generated on a Corporate or Standard calendar by selecting the corresponding radio button, and the weeks can be shown by quarter, month, or week.


Six rows of information will be displayed for every vehicle and daypart that appears on the selected proposals.

  • Current Rate Card Price: shows the price as defined on the rate card for the selected date 
  • Proposal Spot Price: shows the actual average spot price of the proposal for the selected date 
  • Difference: shows the difference between the spot price and the rate card price 
    • If the proposal spot price is less than the rate card price, the figure in the difference column will appear in red preceded by a minus sign .
    • If larger, it will appear black.
    • Green indicates the price for the proposal/daypart is at or above the rate card rate.
  • Current Spot Price to Make Budget: shows the rate at which the remaining inventory must be sold to make the selected budget.
    • Green indicates the price for the proposal/daypart is at or above the rate card rate.
    • Red indicates that the rate is below the rate card rate and will not make budget for the week shown.
  • Resulting Spot Price to Make Budget: shows at what rate the remaining avails will have to be sold to make budget if the proposal is accepted.
    • Green indicates the rate is at or above the rate card rate.
    • Red indicates it is below the rate card rate and will not make budget for the week shown.
  • Difference: shows the difference between the Current Spot Rate to Make Budget and the Resulting Spot Price to Make Budget.
    • If a week is sold out, “Sold Out” will appear in the current and resulting spot price columns.
    • If a vehicle wasn’t ordered on the selected proposals, “0” will appear in the columns.
    • If the current spot price, resulting spot price, or the difference of the two appear in red preceded by a minus sign, this means that the budget goal has been reached for the selected date resolution and the selected budget.


To see the current rate card price compared to the spot price of a proposal without comparing the figures to a budget, you can select a proposal from the box on the left and press tab, without selecting a budget. The columns that compare the proposal to the budget will be left blank.
