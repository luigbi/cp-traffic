Commission Setup

Site Options -> Commissions Tab

You can define the way in which commissions are calculated in the Site Options -> Commissions tab.


A/E Commissions by A/E, Sub-Company, or Contract: This determines how Salespeople’s commissions are calculated


A/E: If commission is calculated by Account Executive, the commissions will be based on the rates entered in each salesperson’s profile


Sub Company: This bases a salesperson’s commission on the Jobs -> Commission screen table. It allows vehicles to be grouped together to by sub-companies defined in the Lists -> Vehicles screen


When the salesperson is defined on the order, you need to define the Sub Company, the Revenue Share % (how the revenue will ‘split’ on the management reports), and the Commission Share % (how much of the revenue will be used to calculate the sales commission).  


In this example, Trish Cox will be shown as the salesperson on the buy and she will get her sales commission on 80% of the revenue for any 24-hour format vehicle that is sold. Jim will also receive sales commission but only 40% of the revenue will be used to calculate his commission amount, and so on.


Contract: Commission % is entered on a contract by contract basis

  • A contract can be shared by up to 10 salespeople for commission or revenue splits.
  • Enter the sales commission for the order
  • The Rev/Commission Share % (if showing) is only changed if a salesperson receives less than the commission on the full net amount



Allow Bonus % for New or Increases Yes or No: Business is considered New if the advertiser is new, or has not had a contract in over a year. If Sales receive different commission rates for new or increases in business, click Yes. If there is a flat rate commission, click No


Commission on Std B’Cast Year or Fiscal: This determines whether your commission is calculated on a Standard Broadcast or Fiscal year

Salespeople

Salespeople are defined in the Lists -> Salespeople screen. 


If the commission rate is the same for all contracts and vehicles, you can enter the base and over goal (if applicable), commission rates for remnant (if applicable), and new and increased business commission rates.


The Job Title field dictates what a user will be able to do throughout the system.

Salesperson
  • Salespeople can only select the Proposal/Order Reports defined with them as the primary salesperson
  • Salespeople do not have the ability to select printable orders
  • The Billing button to allocate promotion or merchandising on the Proposal Screen is disallowed
  • The new Advertiser/Agency screen is modified to require the minimum answer and the accounting questions are skipped
  • When using Projections, they can only enter or modify their own projections
  • The option to use or modify Reservation orders is not shown

Negotiator/Planner

Can do all things that salespeople can do, plus extra capabilities available only to Planners

  • Merchandising
  • Promotion revenue
  • View all Projections
  • Set Projection Blocks
  • Run Projection Roll Overs

Manager

Can do all things that salespeople and Negotiators/Planners can do plus extra capabilities 

  • Approving completed proposals
  • View certain reports

New Client Business

If the client generated revenue two years ago, but not last year, it is considered a new client.  It will remain a new client all through the current year, and all orders placed in the current year will be given the extra commission.  Any orders that span the current year and go into next year will generate the extra commission, but only up to the end of the current year. Any portion of orders for those clients that extend into the following year will not receive the extra commission.  All portions of all orders for new clients will generate the extra commission, including NTR billing.

Rules for calculating Bonus Commission for new business


  1. All orders earning revenue in the selected period will be awarded the Base Commission amount, by multiplying the Current Month Net-Net times the Base Commission % stored in the Salesperson screen for each A/E.
  2. If the “Last Year” column for the advertiser is blank, the Bonus Commission will also be awarded, by multiplying the Current Month Net-Net times the New Client Bonus Commission % stored in the Salesperson screen for each A/E.

Increased Client Business

Increased business is determined by comparing an advertiser’s business this year to the last standard broadcast year (only the last year is compared). Extra commission is calculated on the excess of all orders in the current year, once the total amount exceeds all of last year. Any orders that span the current year and go into next year will generate the extra commission, but only up to the end of the current year. Any portion of orders for those clients that extend into the following year will not receive the extra commission (until next year’s revenue exceeds this year’s revenue).  

Rules for calculating Bonus Commission for increased business


  1. All orders earning revenue in the selected period will be awarded the Base Commission amount, by multiplying the Current Month Net-Net times the Base Commission % stored in the Salesperson screen for each A/E.
  2. If “YTD This Month” is less than “Last Year,” there is no bonus commission.
  3. If “YTD This Month” is greater than “Last Year”, the system subtracts “Last Year” from “YTD This Month” to obtain the commissionable amount, then multiplies the commissionable amount by the Increased Client Bonus Commission.
