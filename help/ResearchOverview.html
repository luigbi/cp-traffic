Research Overview

Research Definitions

Here is a list of basic industry terms:

  • Quantitative research simply counts all people that are listening
  • Qualitative research counts different types of people that are listening, such as people in certain age groups or income groups
  • Population The population is simply the number of people within the area being surveyed
  • Qualitative Population Qualitative research counts different types of people that are listening, such as people in certain age groups, races, or certain income groups, and so on without limit
  • Rating The audience expressed as a percentage of the total population
    • Ratings are found by dividing the number of listeners by the population and multiplying that number by 100
    • Listeners / Population x 100 = Rating (%)
  • AQH (Average Quarterly Hour) Is the average number of people estimated to have listened to a station during any quarter hour during a daypart for at least 5 minutes
    • If an advertiser wants to buy one spot for News Talk 6a-10a and the AQH is 500 then during that daypart the advertiser would reach ~ 500 people
  • AQH Ratings This is AQH expressed as a percentage
    • To find the rating you divide the audience by the population and multiply it by 100
    • AQH/Population x 100 = AQH rating
  • GRP (Gross Ratings Points) The average quarter hour rating point a spot schedule will deliver. AQH Ratings multiplied by the number of spots =GRP
  • GI or GIMP (Gross Impression) Total number of impressions or impacts that have been sold and purchased. AQH  multiplied by the number of spots= GI 
  • CPP ( Cost Per Point) The amount of money needed to reach 1% of the audience. To calculate this you would divide the price by the GRP and that equals CPP.
  • CPM (Cost Per Thousand) The cost to reach 1000 people. To Calculate you take the schedule total multiple it by 1000 and divide by the GI.
  • Reverse CPP This is used to determine what a spot should cost so they can figure out how much each spot should cost
    • If the desired CPP is 80 dollars, and the rating for News Talk is 3, then you would multiple 80 times .3 to arrive at a spot cost of $24


For more terminology, see the Glossary.

Basic Demographic Categories

For each quarter hour and each daypart, there are eighteen standard audience demographic values published, encompassing nine age groups for both men and women.

  

They are: 

Males 12-17

Women 12-17

Males 18-20

Women 18-20

Males 21-24

Women 21-24

Males 25-34

Women 25-34

Males 35-44

Women 35-44

Males 45-49

Women 45-49

Males 50-54

Women 50-54

Males 55-64

Women 55-64

Males 65+

Women 65+


Using the basic 18 audience counts, 132 different values can be calculated, by combining two or more of the basic set. For example, Adults 25-54 can be calculated by adding up men 25-34, women 25-34, men 35-44, women 35-44, men 45-54, and women 45-54.
