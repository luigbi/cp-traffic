Planner Reports

Proposal/Sales

Audience Delivery

Looks at all spots aired for specific dates and calculates the actual audience delivery compared to the audience on the proposal/order and shows the over/under audience differences.  Gross impressions or gross rating points are shown. 

Average Prices to Make Plan

This report calculates what rate the remaining inventory must be sold at in order to make plan. It can be run by week, month, or quarter. The summary version shows the price needed for each daypart by vehicle, and for all dayparts combined. The detail version includes the budget, sold dollars, difference, and price needed for each daypart.  This report uses the dayparts from Rate Card that are defined as Base Dayparts.

CPP/CPM by Demo

Summarizes all orders by the primary demo for each quarter for a year.  Each line shows the primary demo, vehicle advertiser/product, sales office .  Contract information includes total number of thirty second units, gross dollars, average rate and quarterly CPP or CPM.

CPP/CPM by Vehicle

Calculates the CPP or CPM for each daypart within a vehicle.  Up to 13 demos can be selected to see if the demographic goals are being met.  The average rate card price and associated book are also shown.

Inventory Valuation

Inventory version calculates the revenue potential if all spots are sold for the selected rate card.  Available version calculates what the revenue will be from the remaining avails if sold at the selected rate card price.  

Sales Analysis by CPP & CPM

Calculates the CPP & CPM values for any or all demo categories from the primary contract demo for specific dates.  Separate columns are shown for remnants, direct response and PI contracts.

Sales Analysis Summary

This report shows how close vehicles are in reaching budgets by comparing actual, actual with holds or actual with holds and most likely projections.  Tracking is made with the same time last year for one standard or corporate quarter.

Sales Pricing Analysis

Summary or detail options, show how far above or below rate card each salesperson is selling.  These reports can be used to measure salesperson effectiveness in selling at the suggested rate card prices and allowing you to pinpoint which vehicles and dayparts are consistently sold over or under rate card. 

Sales vs. Plan

Summarizes the sales for a vehicle by quarter.  Any budget can be used to compare to the sales.  The value of remaining available inventory is calculated based on the selected rate card. The Base Dayparts are used for this report.

Post Buy Analysis

The net impressions of each spot for paid, mg and bonus is shown for selected dates, selective advertisers.  The overall net impressions is compared against the contract’s guaranteed gross impressions. 

Avails

Avails 

Produces a quarterly summary or detail listing of the available inventory per week for selected vehicles.  Can be run to show avails, sold or percent sold figures.

Avails Pressure

Shows a list of all pending proposals that you can select to combine with existing orders and holds showing what is available for sale.  

Avails Combo by Day/Week

Shows for each vehicle/daypart the total sold and available to sell for 60, 30, 10, etc.  A percent sold and percent available to sell is shown.  The total revenue sold for the spots booked, and the average 30 second and average rate card 30 second spot cost are listed making this report useful for current and historical analysis.

Billed and Booked

All B&B reports show revenue starting from the beginning defined quarter, up to 12 months of totals from the starting quarter as well as quarterly totals.  For standard broadcast month reporting, past data is obtained from actual/billing, future data will be obtained from the contracts.  The last month billed is shown on the top of this report to inform you whether you are looking at billed or projected figures when requested by standard month.


For corporate or calendar reporting, all information will be obtained from contracts with the exception of the Calendar (Spot) option which uses the actual spots to count the revenue. Invoice Adjustment (AN) transaction types are always included with corporate and calendar reporting. 

Billed and Booked Options 

  • Salesperson
    • Option to Show Splits.  When selected will split the revenue based on the order input.
    • Option to Show Sub-totals by vehicle.  When selected will list each vehicle within each salesperson.
    • Can skip to new page for each salesperson.
  • Vehicle Option to subtotal (and page skip) by one of the 5 vehicle groups if defined.  
  • Month Totals:
  • Corporate - Projects all revenue from orders and Includes adjustments
  • Standard Broadcast - Projects future revenue from orders.  Uses A/R for historical revenue and includes invoice adjustments for all advertisers equal or prior to last invoiced date.
  • Calendar - Projects all revenue from orders and includes invoice adjustments for all advertisers equal or prior to last invoiced date.
  • Calendar (Spots) - Spot revenue is obtained from the scheduled and missed spots if included.  Invoice adjustments are included for all advertisers equal or prior to last invoiced date.
  • Revenue Totals: Gross, Net, T-Net (is net less): Acquisition cost; Merchandising; Promotion;  Acquisition Cost Only option.
  • Include Status of Orders: Hold and/or Order
  • Include Type of Order: Standard, Reserved, Remnant, Direct Response, PI, PSA, Promo, NTR

Billed and Booked Comparison

The dual sort feature on this report allows you to customize the way you see the revenue based on your sort preferences.


You can select a budget from the current or previous year for comparisons.  There are also figures for cumulative YTD sales, budget and percentage budget to sales.  

Billed and Booked Recap

This report shows direct vs. agency order revenue.  Agency revenue is determined by any order that has an agency and advertiser.  Direct revenue is determined by those orders that have no agencies.  The Sales Origin totals for each Sales Source are shown.  The Sales Origin totals are local, regional or national.

NTR Billed and Booked

Produces a report of 12 months NTR/Hard Cost Revenue.  For standard broadcast month reporting, past data will be obtained from actual/billing, future data will be obtained from the contracts.  For corporate reporting, all information will be obtained from contracts.  For Corporate Reporting, any invoice adjustments (AN) transaction types will also be included.

Multimedia Billed and Booked

Produces a report of 12 months NTR/Hard Cost Revenue.  For standard broadcast month reporting, past data will be obtained from actual/billing, future data will be obtained from the contracts.  For corporate reporting, all information will be obtained from contracts.  For Corporate Reporting, any invoice adjustments (AN) transaction types will also be included.

Pacing Reports

Pacing reports provide a ‘snapshot’ of the orders on the books for any given date.  The database maintains a history of all changes made to orders, and these reports can look at the version in effect for any specified date. There are eight pacing reports in the system that bring together research, budgets, actuals, orders on the books, rate card, and projections for future business.


  1. Actual/Projection Comparison
  2. Advertiser Variance
  3. Billed and Booked
  4. *CPP/CPM by Demo
  5. Custom Revenue
  6. Tie Out (by Sales Office or Vehicle)
  7. *Sales Analysis Summary
  8. Upfront/Scatter
