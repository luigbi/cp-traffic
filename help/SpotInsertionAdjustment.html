Spot Insertion Adjustment

On version 7.1 and above, on the X-Digital Spot Insertions export, the StartDate and EndDate fields will get altered under certain circumstances to correct for adjustments that the XDS system makes depending on the head end time zone and station time zone. This applies to HB, HBP, and ISCI avail forms.


StartDate and EndDate example:


<StartDate>01/13/2020 00:00:00</StartDate>

<EndDate>01/13/2020 23:59:59</EndDate>


The XDS system that receives Spot Insertions exported from Counterpoint makes adjustments to those StartDate and EndDate dates and times using the head end zone and station time zone, adding or subtracting dates and hours depending on the amount of change. For example, if the head end is Eastern, and the station time zone is Pacific, it would adjust the above example as follows:


<StartDate>01/12/2020 21:00:00</StartDate>

<EndDate>01/13/2020 20:59:59</EndDate>

It's subtracting three hours from the StartDate, changing 1/13/20 00:00:00 to 1/12/20 21:00:00, and it's subtracting three hours from the EndDate, changing 1/13/20 23:59:59 to 1/13/20 20:59:59.


When using the XDS import to import airing information direction from XDS, from the example above, if a spot was airing on 1/13/20 after 9pm on this Pacific time zone station, the air date would be set to 1/12/20 instead of 1/13/20 when it gets imported to the web spot database from the XDS import.


To make the air date that gets imported correct, the XDS export sent from the Affiliate system adjusts the StartDate and EndDate using the head end time zone (from Traffic Site Options) and the station time zone (from Stations) so that the date will be correct when it gets imported from XDS. If the head end is Eastern, and the station time zone is Pacific, it will add three hours to the StartDate and EndDate as it gets exported from the Affiliate system using the XDS export. For an Eastern head end, Mountain time zone station, it will add two hours. For an Eastern head end, Central time zone station, it will add one hour. For an Eastern head end and Eastern time zone station, there will be no adjustment. (Non-Eastern head end zones will also be adjusted as needed.)


For example, for an Eastern head end, and Pacific time zone station, instead of this: 


<StartDate>01/13/2020 00:00:00</StartDate>

<EndDate>01/13/2020 23:59:59</EndDate>


The StartDate and EndDate will be:


<StartDate>01/13/2020 03:00:00</StartDate>

<EndDate>01/14/2020 02:59:59</EndDate>


So when it gets exported from the head end, which subtracts three hours, it will be sent to the station as:


<StartDate>01/13/2020 00:00:00</StartDate>

<EndDate>01/13/2020 23:59:59</EndDate>


Additional note: if the head end zone is blank, it will be treated as Eastern.
