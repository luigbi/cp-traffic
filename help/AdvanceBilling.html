Advance Billing

Advance billing can be performed for installment type contracts (version 7.1 and above). To use this feature, all the installment billing on a contract must be allocated to a single month, which must be either the same month that the contract starts, or prior to the start of the contract. 


For example, the current billing period is December, and there’s an installment contract that is being entered with air time/NTR that starts in June and runs through July. To bill this contract in advance, allocate the installment revenue to a single month, whichever month you wish to bill it in. For example, if you wish to bill it in December, allocate all the installment revenue to January. It you wish to bill it in June, allocate all the installment revenue to June. 


In the picture below, all the installment revenue has been allocated to June. This contract will be billed in full, in June, with the second month invoice being just a list of air time spots and/or NTR.


In the example below, a “start invoice date” of 12/27/15 was entered, even though the contract itself starts in June. Because all the installment revenue is allocated to a single month in advance of the contract start date (which is in June), it will be billed in advance, in December 2015.


The invoice example below shows an invoice example from an installment contract that was billed in advance. None of the spots have aired, so there are no spots to show. Only the header portion and the amount due is shown (at the bottom of the page).


Continuing with the example, later, when June and July are invoiced, invoices will be created that show the spots and/or NTR, but the revenue itself will have already been billed in advance, so that won’t be shown on the June and July invoices (see June invoice example below).
